01Vision

We are not building a better group app. We are building the first tool where you describe software to your friends and it simply exists — private, calm, and quietly intelligent — with no company in the middle.

Almost every group product optimizes for time-on-app: notifications, feeds, streaks, badges that nag. Rooms makes a series of decisions that reject that entire model:

Dumb relay

The server only moves encrypted blobs. We cannot surveil users even if we wanted to.

Local-first + E2E

The data is theirs, on their devices. We hold ciphertext we cannot read.

No accounts

No profile to mine, no funnel to optimize, no PII to leak.

No push

We don't interrupt your life. Awareness happens only when you open the app.

AI builds the apps

Describe a room → it exists. The "wow" is one sentence away.

Calm, not addictive

We monetize creation and value, never attention.

Individually these are engineering choices. Together they are a philosophy — and that philosophy is the moat. Incumbents can't copy "we can't read your data and we won't notify you" without breaking their own business model.

02The product & the hero moment

One PWA. One dumb relay. Many room templates — built-in (ChoreBoard, Trip Split, Book Club, FitCrew) and declarative mini-apps anyone (or AI) can spin up from building blocks.

The one magical moment to obsess over

You type: "a place where my 4 roommates vote on what to cook this week" 3 seconds: the app exists, on screen, working You tap: Share → roommates are using it in 30 seconds, no signup

Speak-it-into-existence + zero-friction-join. No other product does both. Notion needs accounts. ChatGPT can't make a shared multiplayer thing. Glide/Bubble need logins and dashboards. We do it in a sentence and a link.

The growth loop is the product

We don't need ads. The room is the marketing:

make a room → share a link → friend joins (no signup) → friend sees how easy it was → friend makes their own room → repeat

The calm stance helps virality: people share things they trust and are proud of. They don't share the app that nags their friends.

The discipline The hero moment cannot require pasting an API key (see §03). And the building blocks must get richer fast (dates, numbers, money, reminders) or the "build anything" promise breaks on contact with real use.

03AI strategy — two workloads, opposite economics

The mistake is treating "AI in Rooms" as one thing. It's two, and conflating them is what created the API-key friction problem.

Generation (the hero moment)Operation (ongoing helper)
WhatOne sentence → room schema JSONWeekly suggestions, digests, summaries
FrequencyOnce per room + a few retriesRecurring, per room, forever
TokensTiny, one-shot, structuredLarger context, repeated
Touches private room data?No — just "design me a chore app"Yes — reads members' content
Who paysWe subsidize it (it's basically free)BYOK / Ollama, or a paid tier

The cost math: generation is nearly free

A schema generation is the cheapest kind of AI call — small system prompt, tiny input, short structured output: ≈ 2,000 tokens per generation. At a cheap fast tier (~$0.15/1M in, ~$0.60/1M out):

VolumeApprox cost
1 generation~$0.0006 (6 hundredths of a cent)
1 user × 10 retries~$0.006
10,000 users × 10 each~$60
1,000,000 generations~$600

We can give every new user ~15 free "spells" and the entire hero-moment experience is rounding-error money. The friction was self-inflicted: we only reached for BYOK because we assumed all AI must be BYOK. It doesn't.

Why a generation proxy does not betray "the relay is dumb"

We can run two backends with completely different trust profiles. They don't contaminate each other:

DEVICE (local-first, E2E) BACKENDS ┌────────────────────────┐ ┌───────────────────────────────────┐ │ Rooms PWA │◀────▶│ RELAY — DUMB │ │ Room content (Yjs/IDB) │ enc. │ sees only ciphertext · the backbone│ └──────────┬─────────────┘ blobs└───────────────────────────────────┘ │ "make a chore voting app" ▼ ┌───────────────────────────────────┐ │ GENERATION PROXY — STATELESS │ schema JSON ◀───────────│ holds platform AI key · no DB │ │ no accounts · never room content │ └───────────────────────────────────┘
The privacy line that protects the brand Generation never touches private data. "A chore app where roommates vote" is an app description the user chooses to send to AI — not E2E room content. The schema returned is a public-ish app definition. Routing it through a stateless function leaks nothing our encryption promise covers. The relay stays dumb and ciphertext-only; the proxy is an optional faucet, not a vault.

The ambient room helper (no extra server)

Cloud AI can't reach a room while everyone is offline — but rooms are social, so somebody usually opens the app. Store AI intents in the room CRDT ("every Friday, propose 3 ideas"). When any member opens the app and background sync runs, their device checks for a due task, claims it via a CRDT lock, runs it (BYOK on-device, or via the proxy for paid users), and writes results back. Your friends' devices are the cloud. Ambient, scheduled-feeling AI with zero servers — fully private.

Calm + AI = value-per-open, not opens-per-day The deepest risk to calm software is being forgotten. The fix: the AI prepares value silently, so your rare visits are delightful. We already ship the awareness layer (background sync + "Updated" badges). The helper makes each open worth it: the weekend options are already drafted, the changes already summarized. Open occasionally → something good is waiting → leave. The healthiest loop in software.

04Data privacy — the honest, and better, story

Yes: the relay holds the encrypted current state of every room at all times (in RAM, and on disk if persistence is on). That sounds like a liability. The honest framing turns it into the strongest part of the pitch.

The reality (no spin)

  • The relay stores room.log per room — after checkpoint compaction, usually one encrypted blob (the current document), not full history.
  • This is needed so an offline device or a brand-new member can catch up even when no other peer is online. Pure peer-to-peer would break async groups (a chore board checked once a day).
  • So yes — we hold all rooms' data. But it is ciphertext we cannot decrypt. We hold no keys.
The reframe that fixes everything: the relay is a cache, not a vault The canonical copy of a room lives on members' devices. The relay is a convenience rendezvous so peers who are never online at the same time can still sync. This single idea improves the privacy story, the deletion story, and the biggest cost driver (RAM) — all at once.

Zero-access encryption

Keys derive from the room code, which never reaches the relay — only a one-way SHA-256-derived room id does. A breach yields ciphertext without keys: noise. This is the same model trusted password managers and encrypted backups use ("zero-knowledge").

Data that dies when you stop caring

Because the relay is a cache, we can let it forget:

Room stateRelay behavior
Active (recent connections)In RAM + disk for instant catch-up
Idle for N daysEvict from RAM, keep small blob on disk
Idle for N weeks/monthsDelete from disk entirely
A member reconnects laterSelf-healing: their device re-seeds the relay with a checkpoint
Nobody holds a copy anymoreData is simply gone — by design

This is a beautiful, honest line: your data outlives our servers and dies when you all let it go.

The right to destroy

  • Explicit delete: a room owner sends a signed tombstone (authorized by the admin secret) → relay purges the room file + RAM. Needs care because the relay has no accounts; the admin-secret signature is the authorization.
  • Natural expiry: idle-room eviction above gives erasure for free.
  • Local wipe: already supported on-device ("Remove from this device"). Combined with relay expiry, the data evaporates everywhere.

Honest limitations (say these out loud)

Metadata is visible

The relay sees hashed room ids, connection times, IPs, blob sizes. Mitigate: don't log IPs, minimal retention. Content stays encrypted.

The room code is the key

Anyone with the invite code can read & join. Protect links. We never see the code (only a one-way hash).

No public-channel revocation

Removing a member doesn't re-key the public room; anyone who had the code can still read. Admin channel can rotate its secret.

Generation prompt leaves the device

App-design prompts go to the proxy/AI — never room content. BYOK/Ollama avoids even that.

Compliance is light by design

No accounts, no PII, no plaintext → strong data minimization. Right to erasure satisfied by delete + eviction. Breach-notification risk is low (encrypted, no identities). This is a far lighter compliance burden than typical SaaS, and a real trust asset for clubs, families, and privacy-conscious users.

How to communicate it "We store encrypted blocks we cannot read. Your devices are the source of truth — our servers are just a cache. Your data self-destructs when you let it, and you can delete a room anytime. A breach of our servers gives an attacker noise." Contrast with Big Tech: they hold plaintext, profile you, and can't truly delete. We hold ciphertext, can't profile, and the data dies with your attention.

05Operational cost — where the big items really are

The instinct "we'll need many servers holding everyone's data" overestimates the hard part. Because the relay is a cache (not the source of truth), durability is cheap and the only real pressure is live RAM and connections.

Cost itemDriverSeverityLever
RAMCurrent code loads every persisted room into memory at startupHigh — #1 itemLazy-load on connect + evict idle rooms. Caps RAM at active rooms, not all-rooms-ever.
Concurrency / connectionsOne WebSocket per active peer; background sync adds brief burstsMediumGo handles many cheaply; shard by room id across relays when needed.

DiskOne small blob file per roomLow1M rooms × 100KB ≈ 100GB ≈ a few $/mo. Tiered + expiry.
Durability / backupsRelay disk failureLowMembers' devices are the backup. Relays can be semi-disposable caches — no expensive replication needed.

AI ambient helperRecurring per-room tasksLowDevice-hosted/BYOK = $0 to us. Platform-hosted ≈ $0.01–0.02/room/month.
Multi-region / shardingGlobal scale, latencyLaterRoom-id sharding; geo relays via relay-url override. Not needed pre-PMF.

The RAM picture, concretely

Today (all rooms resident): 1,000,000 rooms × 100KB ≈ 100 GB RAM ← expensive With eviction (active only): ~1–5% active ≈ 1–5 GB RAM ← cheap

Eviction is safe because of self-healing: if a member reconnects to an evicted room, their client sends a checkpoint and re-populates the relay. The work is already half-done — checkpoints exist; we just need lazy-load + an idle sweeper.

The cost reframe for investors and ops We are not running a database of everyone's data. We're running a stateless-ish cache in front of millions of self-backing devices. That makes us cheap to operate, cheap to scale horizontally, and resilient to data loss — the canonical data isn't ours to lose.

06Monetization

Charge for the magic and the convenience — at the highest-intent moment (creation) — never for attention. Users self-select into three lanes.

FREE

Everyone

~15 free generations. The frictionless front door. Costs us cents. This is the growth engine, not a loss leader to minimize.

BYOK / OLLAMA

Developers, privacy folks

Unlimited generation + on-device ambient helper. $0 to us. High setup friction — but this group wants it.

ROOMS PLUS · ~$3–5/mo

Mainstream fans

Unlimited generation + platform-hosted ambient helper, one tap, no key. AI cost ≈ pennies/user → fat margins.

Unit economics (Plus)

  • Heavy user with 10 active helper rooms: ~$0.20/month AI cost against a $3–5 sub.
  • Only way this gets expensive: big models, huge context, streaming chat — none of which this product needs.
  • Relay marginal cost per user is dominated by RAM of active rooms, which eviction keeps tiny.

Abuse control without accounts

  • Constrain output to a valid RoomSchema + hard token cap → useless as a free general LLM.
  • Sign generation requests with the existing persona keypair → rate-limit per public key (no accounts needed).
  • Edge IP rate limits + a device-side free-generation counter for the honest majority.

08Risks & honest watch-outs

Messaging the privacy line

"Local-first & E2E" but "generation uses our proxy" must be crystal clear (app definitions ≠ room content). Muddle it and we lose the trust moat. Load-bearing copywriting.

Conversion, not acquisition

Calm products see lower daily engagement → fewer paywall hits. Counter: paywall create and helper, the high-intent moments.

Thin building blocks

Today: text, tags, votes, status. The promise breaks the moment someone needs dates, money, RSVPs. Richer bricks are the #1 roadmap item after in-app generation.

Cold-start loneliness

A one-person room is sad. Push toward "share now" and seed AI starter content so a joining friend sees life, not a blank list.

Free-generation abuse

Manageable (schema-only output, signed requests, rate limits), but real. Monitor.

Calm ≠ forgotten

Solved by value-per-open (ambient helper + digests), but must actually ship, or retention suffers.

09Next steps

Ordered by leverage. Each builds on what already ships (background sync + "Updated" badges are done).

  1. Write ROOM_AI.md — lock the two-workload split, the privacy boundary, the stateless proxy contract (schema-validated output, persona-signed, rate-limited), and the Free/BYOK/Plus lanes. Agree before any code.
  2. Stateless generation proxy — one edge function holding the platform key; the hero moment, frictionless, no API key. BYOK wired as the power-user fallback.
  3. In-app "speak it into existence" — type a sentence → schema appears live → create → share. Replace copy-paste-to-ChatGPT as the front door.
  4. Richer building blocks — add date, number, money, assignee, reminder bricks (forward-compatible via existing schemaVersion/engineVersion).
  5. Relay: lazy-load + idle eviction — cap RAM at active rooms; rely on self-healing checkpoints. The #1 cost lever.
  6. Room delete (signed tombstone) — admin-secret-authorized purge on the relay; "right to destroy" as a visible feature.
  7. Ambient room helper — AI intents in the CRDT, device-claimed and executed; "while you were away" digests on open.
  8. Position & name around the stance — calm, sovereign, conjure-an-app. The product is ready for a bigger story than "rooms for small groups."
The one-line bet The legendary version isn't "a better group app." It's the first tool where you describe software to your friends and it simply exists — private, calm, and quietly intelligent — with no company in the middle. The architecture is already shaped like that product. The work is finishing the hero moment and telling the story out loud.

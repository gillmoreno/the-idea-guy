01The principle: the viral surface is free, the costly surface is paid

Rooms splits cleanly into two surfaces with opposite economics. The whole pricing strategy is: never confuse them.

SurfaceWhat's on itMarginal cost to usPolicy
Viral surface Joining rooms, using rooms, sharing links/QR, built-in templates, catalog rooms, number of rooms & members ≈ $0 (once relay eviction ships, a room costs ~100KB of RAM only while active) Free forever, never gated. Every limit here is a hole in the growth loop.
Costly surface AI generation (one-shot, ~$0.0006/call) and the ambient helper (recurring, ~$0.01–0.02/room/mo platform-hosted) Real but tiny — and entirely AI, not infra Metered free taste, then paid. The only place money is charged is the only place money is spent.
Why this resolves the virality-vs-budget tension The thing that spreads the product (join → use → share → make your own) and the thing that costs money (AI calls) are different features. So the free tier can be unlimited on every viral dimension without the bill scaling with virality. A million people joining rooms costs roughly one VPS; a million people generating is the only thing that scales spend — and that is capped, rate-limited, and cheap per unit.

The headline At 10,000 monthly actives the expected bill is ~$30/mo — less than one Plus subscriber per thousand users needs to cover. The platform is structurally cheap because the expensive thing (storing and serving everyone's data) is done by the users' own devices.
The two things that would break this table (1) Relay without eviction: RAM scales with all-rooms-ever instead of active rooms — that's why it's a hard launch gate. All numbers are pre-launch estimates. Phase 0 (§07) exists to replace them with measured generations-per-user, %-creators, and real room sizes.

04Free tier design — the virality engine

Unlimited, forever, never gated

Joining, using, sharing, built-in templates, catalog rooms, room count, member count. No accounts, no trials, no watermarks on the viral path.

15 free "spells" (AI generations)

Enough to feel the magic several times and build 2–3 real custom rooms. Device-counted + persona-signed (rate-limitable without accounts). Lifetime cost ceiling: ~$0.009/user.

Viral refill: earn spells by growing the product

+5 spells when a new persona joins a room you generated (capped, e.g. +30/mo). The scarce resource is paid out for exactly the behavior that spreads Rooms — and each reward costs us ~$0.003. Growth and generosity point the same direction.

BYOK / Ollama: the pressure valve

Unlimited everything on your own key or hardware, $0 to us. Kills the "greedy paywall" criticism before it exists and serves the privacy audience that markets us.

Design intent A free user should never hit a wall while using or sharing Rooms — only while creating new software with our AI money. By the time someone exhausts 15+ spells they have made several rooms, invited friends, and proven intent: that's the conversion moment, not an annoyance.

05Why people pay — the incentive has to be recurring

"Unlimited generations" alone is a weak subscription: creation is bursty, and a one-time need converts once then churns. The strong incentive is the ambient helper — the only feature with ongoing marginal cost is also the only one with ongoing value.

FREE

Where conversion happens (high-intent moments only)

  • The 16th spell — "you've built 3 rooms your friends use; keep conjuring for $4/mo (or plug in your own key for free)".
  • The helper toggle — "let AI keep this room fresh every week" inside a room people already love. This is the moment recurring value is obvious.
  • Never on join, never on open, never via nag — calm stance applies to monetization too.

Price point

Anchor at $4/mo or $40/yr (test $3 vs $5 later — at 90% margins, conversion rate matters far more than the digit). One paid tier only; complexity is a tax on a solo operator.

06Budget safety rails — the bill is a chosen number

"I don't have infinite money" is a design constraint, so the worst case must be engineered, not hoped for.

Net effect Fixed floor ≈ one VPS + domains (~$15–30/mo). Variable ceiling ≈ the proxy spend cap you choose. Everything else is structurally $0. A viral moment can max out the generation budget for the month — it cannot generate a surprise invoice.

07Rollout sequence — don't charge before the recurring value exists

  1. Phase 0 · Friends & family (now): everything free, BYOK wired. Measure: generations per creator, % of users who create vs join, room sizes, retention per template. These numbers replace every guess in §02–§03.
  2. Phase 1 · Public launch: Free (15 spells + viral refill) + BYOK. No paid tier yet — charging for unlimited generations alone is a weak offer, and an empty paywall at launch dampens the viral loop precisely when it matters most. Optional: an "early supporter" tip with a founder badge, grandfathered into Plus later.
  3. Phase 2 · Rooms Plus (when the ambient helper ships): $4/mo at the two high-intent moments (§05). The helper is the reason the subscription exists; launching them together makes the offer honest.
The discipline this requires Phase 1 will earn $0 by design. The temptation to bolt on a paywall early should be answered with: conversion needs recurring value, recurring value needs the helper, and meanwhile the entire platform costs less per month than a dinner out (§03).

08Open decisions (Gil's call)

  • Proxy spend cap: the worst-case monthly AI number — $25? $50? $100?
  • Spell count: 15 is the working number; phase 0 data may say 10 or 25.
  • Viral refill mechanic: in or out? (Recommended in — it aligns the scarce resource with growth; needs anti-gaming caps: unique personas only, per-room and per-month limits.)
  • Price point: $3 / $4 / $5 — recommend $4, decide before Phase 2 only.
  • Proxy model/provider: any cheap fast tier works at ~2k tokens/call (Haiku-class, Flash-class); pick on JSON reliability, not price — the call is $0.0006 either way.
  • Relay hosting: Hetzner-class VPS (cheapest, generous bandwidth) vs Fly.io (easier multi-region later). Either fits the §03 table.
